Introduction

Wood pellets fuel is considered to be a valuable tool for climate mitigation. The fuel source was designated carbon neutral by the European Union in 2009, South Korea in 2012, and Japan in 2020. As a result, there was a spike in demand for wood pellets from the United States. By 2020, nearly 7.26 million metric tons of wood pellets were exported from the US, representing a roughly one-billion-dollar export market. However, this increase in tree harvesting and pellet production has received considerable pushback from environmental NGOs and southeastern American communities. New production plants have promised economic growth, while activists have highlighted how the ecological impacts and health effects negatively impact rural communities where plants are located. This proposal sets forth a plan for an interactive web map to visualize environmental justice concerns to explore the environmental justice impacts of a set of proposed wood pellet plants in central Arkansas.

  1. Purpose of the map

    The study area will be in central Arkansas, with three proposed wood pellet facilities run by Drax. This UK-based power company uses wood pellets to deliver carbon-neutral power to homes across the Atlantic. The three sites are between the Ozark and Ouachita National forest zones. With major urban center Little Rock nearby, the focus will be on these satellite plants' communities. The first data point will be to understand the reported cases of asthma in each census tract. The wood industry had existed in the area long before these plants broke ground. So we need to understand the existing cases and other relevant lung conditions. This variable makes sense as biomass plants in Massachusetts have been shut down after these indicators were fully understood. The next indicator I will look at is average income. Areas selected for pellet mill siting are found in low-income areas. Finally, I will use air quality data to identify Volatile Organic Compound levels (VOC) and Particulate Material (PM2.5) to identify areas that are already at risk. These variables are essential as they show the high environmental health risk associated with wood pellet production.

  2. Structure of the map

    The map will show the contiguous United States with state and national borders indicated. For each state and subdivision, zooming capability will allow the user to understand the quantity of pellets produced in that region. An interactive chart within the website will allow users to sort by individual site, seeing expected use capacity. There is also an opportunity to include qualitative data that shows which communities have voiced support or have pushed back against these facilities.

  3. Data Sources

    I will draw on several key data sources. First, I will use census data to identify population blocks and other key demographic data. I will then combine federal and state public health and air quality data sets to identify rates of asthma and other illnesses. Finally, I will pull economic data by state to show wood pellet production levels and economic value. For the non-data analytics focused data, I will develop a shapefile of current and proposed wood pellet facilities. Should time permit, I will identify local power generation facilites that use wood pellets to generate electricity.
